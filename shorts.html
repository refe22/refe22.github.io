Post 1: Footsteps

Originally written in 2019

I have a chain about my mouth. A ball and chain. It sags my lips to the floor. The wooden floor with splintering pricks I step on some times because they haven’t sanded the ground. I feel it, in my heart I know I think about it too much. It is a hovering curse, a never-ending cycle of pure insanity. What has my life become? Unknowingly I surrendered my life, my everything to you—the one person who I could never trust. How did this start? And more importantly, when will this end? I see you in the people walking by. I never knew before that I paid attention to how people walk. It so interesting how the pattern in which one foot steps in front of another could contribute to identifying a person. You walk with a sort of sway, feet farther apart than your hip length. Your toes angle your foot out. “You walk like a penguin” I’d say. To this, you would never reply, you go on swaying, picking up the pace every few seconds. “When do you go to breakfast?” “Seven” I’d say without missing a second. You pause for a second, I wonder to myself if I told you the truth. “We could meet then for breakfast.” I let myself smile a bit then. “Sure.” It’s funny the things I do remember about you: how your eyes narrow even someone says something you don’t understand or how fast you walk. It’s those feet, those **** feet I swear! “Why do you walk so fast?” “Why do you walk so slow?” I would only try to match your pace as you waddled onward. I wonder now if you ever think about me. Do you remember how I look at you? I don’t remember never bursting into laughter or smiling so wide whenever I saw you. “Could I tell you something?” I was real nervous then, you were making a face I couldn’t recognize. “Sure.” You didn’t sound that inviting. We were just there standing outside your room door. It never felt hotter in California before then. Would you care if I were gone? “What is it?” My mouth, I noticed was still plastered shut. Did you ever love me? Did you ever think of me? Did you ever notice the way that I walk? “Could you sign this? It’s for a birthday card.” “Sure!” There was a face I recognized. Brown, almost black eyes, straight posture, toes pointed out in a waddle. You take the card, I take my ball and chain. My feet take me away for you and I must of done before. I listen for the door to close before I finally cry.

-March 5, 2019
